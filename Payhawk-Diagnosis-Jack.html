The Core Leak

Payhawk runs two acquisition tracks: self-serve trial for businesses under 20 employees, and sales-led demos for enterprise. The middle is empty.

Mid-market companies — 50 to 500 employees — represent the highest volume growth opportunity in spend management. They are too large for the Growth plan at £149 per month and too small to warrant dedicated enterprise sales attention. No nurture sequence catches them. No Account Based Marketing cadence moves them from engagement to pipeline. No demand gen playbook converts the awareness the brand campaigns generate into repeatable, measurable pipeline.

The Global Demand Generation Manager job posting lists Account Based Marketing strategy and playbook development as core responsibilities — not management of existing systems. The infrastructure does not exist yet. Every month it does not exist, awareness turns into nothing.

The gap is not brand. The gap is the system that converts brand into named account pipeline.
What This Seems to Indicate

Brand investment without demand capture is a leaky bucket

Payhawk invested in category-level brand building — outdoor advertising, G2 reviews, product marketing. But without an Account Based Marketing layer converting awareness into named account engagement, the spend funds Ramp’s pipeline. Prospects see Payhawk, then search “spend management software,” where Ramp owns every top organic result.

The enterprise motion is outpacing the infrastructure supporting it

Enterprise customers exist across automotive, hospitality, and professional services. The majority of the 51 customer stories skew mid-market. The job posting lists “Winning Named Accounts” and “Engaged-to-Sales-Accepted-Lead conversion” as goals, not current metrics. The measurement framework for enterprise demand gen does not exist yet.

Every campaign starts from zero

462 employees across 9 offices. When a new market launches or a new product line needs pipeline, there is no playbook to pull off the shelf. Each campaign requires full ideation, build, and optimisation — an estimated 30 to 40 percent efficiency loss versus playbook-driven operations.

Competitor content is winning the mid-funnel war

Ramp publishes comparison content at scale and offers their platform free. Spendesk owns European search terms Payhawk does not rank for. Neither has Payhawk’s customer proof or European multi-currency depth, but they are capturing the research-phase buyer that Payhawk’s brand campaigns create.

Every quarter without a demand gen engine, an estimated 10 to 15 enterprise prospects engage with Payhawk through G2, outdoor advertising, or word-of-mouth and receive no follow-up. No Account Based Marketing sequence. No nurture cadence. At €75,000 average enterprise deal size, that represents over €1.5 million in pipeline that awareness created but nobody captured.

What MDB Runs First
1

Run the Account Based Marketing measurement framework as live infrastructure

Every campaign needs attribution from the start. MDB defines the metrics, instruments the tracking, and operates the measurement layer on a running basis — not as a setup task, but as the ongoing infrastructure every campaign reports into.

Produces: Every campaign is measurable, improvable, and attributable. The fundraise story has pipeline data behind it.
2

Deploy the 51 customer stories as industry-specific Account Based Marketing assets

Automotive savings, hospitality time recovery, professional services VAT recapture — these are not testimonials. They are vertical-specific pipeline fuel. MDB runs them as named account sequences by industry, reaching the right companies with peer proof before competitors do.

Produces: Outbound that lands because it leads with verified outcomes, not product features.
3

Build the comparison content that captures high-intent organic search

“Spend management software” is owned by Ramp. “Expense management platform” is owned by Navan and Concur. Payhawk has stronger European multi-currency and VAT capabilities than either, but does not appear when buyers search. MDB builds the comparison pages and pillar content that close that gap permanently.

Produces: Mid-funnel demand capture that compounds over time — not paid campaigns that stop when the budget does.
